<p><b>Parliamentary elections</b> were held in <a href="/wiki/Montenegro" title="Montenegro">Montenegro</a> on 30 August 2020.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> They were the eleventh <a href="/wiki/General_election" title="General election">parliamentary elections</a> since the first multi-party elections in&#160;1990, and the fifth in Montenegro since <a href="/wiki/2006_Montenegrin_independence_referendum" title="2006 Montenegrin independence referendum">gaining its independence in 2006</a>. Eighty-one members of the&#160;<a href="/wiki/Parliament_of_Montenegro" title="Parliament of Montenegro">Montenegrin parliament</a> were elected. Elections were organized in special conditions, due to the <a href="/wiki/COVID-19_pandemic_in_Montenegro" title="COVID-19 pandemic in Montenegro">COVID-19 pandemic in Montenegro</a>. The parliamentary election was also held simultaneously with the <a href="/wiki/2020_Montenegrin_municipal_elections" title="2020 Montenegrin municipal elections">local elections in five municipalities</a>.
</p><p>The period before the election was marked by the high polarization of the electorate. Several corruption scandals of the ruling party triggered <a href="/wiki/2019_Montenegrin_anti-corruption_protests" title="2019 Montenegrin anti-corruption protests">2019 anti-government protests</a>, while a controversial <a href="/wiki/Freedom_of_religion_in_Montenegro" title="Freedom of religion in Montenegro">religion law</a> sparked another <a href="/wiki/2019%E2%80%932020_Clerical_protests_in_Montenegro" class="mw-redirect" title="2019–2020 Clerical protests in Montenegro">wave of protests</a>. 
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Election_monitoring" title="Election monitoring">Election observers</a> <a href="/wiki/OSCE" class="mw-redirect" title="OSCE">Organization for Security and Co-operation in Europe</a> stated: "Abuse of state resources gave the <a href="/wiki/Ruling_party" title="Ruling party">ruling party</a> an unfair advantage", and said that although the elections were competitive, the governing party also benefited from a lack of <a href="/wiki/Independent_media" title="Independent media">independent media</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The election resulted in a victory for the <a href="/wiki/Parliamentary_opposition" title="Parliamentary opposition">opposition parties</a> and the fall from power of the ruling <a href="/wiki/Democratic_Party_of_Socialists" class="mw-redirect" title="Democratic Party of Socialists">DPS</a>, which had ruled the country since the introduction of the <a href="/wiki/Multi-party_system" title="Multi-party system">multi-party system</a> in 1990. On 31 August, the leaders of three opposition coalitions, <a href="/wiki/For_the_Future_of_Montenegro" title="For the Future of Montenegro">For the Future of Montenegro</a>, <a href="/wiki/Peace_is_Our_Nation" title="Peace is Our Nation">Peace is Our Nation</a> and <a href="/wiki/United_Reform_Action" title="United Reform Action">In Black and White</a>, agreed to form an <a href="/wiki/Technocracy" title="Technocracy">expert government</a>, and to continue to work on the <a href="/wiki/Accession_of_Montenegro_to_the_European_Union" title="Accession of Montenegro to the European Union">European Union accession process</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Background">Background</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2020_Montenegrin_parliamentary_election&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Following the <a href="/wiki/2016_Montenegrin_parliamentary_election" title="2016 Montenegrin parliamentary election">2016 parliamentary elections</a>, the entire <a href="/wiki/Opposition_(Montenegro)#10th_assembly" title="Opposition (Montenegro)">opposition</a> started a collective <a href="/wiki/Boycott" title="Boycott">boycott</a> of all parliamentary sittings. In January 2017, 39 of the 81 MPs were boycotting parliament, requesting early elections to be held no later than 2018, when the next <a href="/wiki/2018_Montenegrin_presidential_election" title="2018 Montenegrin presidential election">presidential elections</a> were scheduled.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> 
</p><p>In 2016, then Vice President of the ruling <a href="/wiki/Democratic_Party_of_Socialists" class="mw-redirect" title="Democratic Party of Socialists">DPS</a> <a href="/wiki/Svetozar_Marovi%C4%87" title="Svetozar Marović">Svetozar Marović</a> was arrested in connection to a long-running corruption case concerning his hometown of <a href="/wiki/Budva" title="Budva">Budva</a>; the Montenegrin prosecutor's office labeled him as "head of Budva criminal group," which he later admitted in court. He eventually fled to neighboring <a href="/wiki/Serbia" title="Serbia">Serbia</a> for alleged psychiatric treatment in <a href="/wiki/Belgrade" title="Belgrade">Belgrade</a>, where he currently resides. Montenegro has repeatedly requested his extradition from Serbia.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> In August 2020, Marović spoke to the media for the first time, after fleeing to Belgrade, accusing the leadership of the party he founded of corruption, nepotism, <a href="/wiki/Particracy" title="Particracy">partocracy</a> and authoritarianism, also accusing <a href="/wiki/Milo_%C4%90ukanovi%C4%87" title="Milo Đukanović">President Đukanović</a> of rigging the corruption process against him and members of his family.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>In its June 2018 report, issued after the April 2018 presidential election, the <a href="/wiki/Organization_for_Security_and_Co-operation_in_Europe" title="Organization for Security and Co-operation in Europe">OSCE</a>’s Office for Democratic Institutions and Human Rights, <a href="/wiki/Office_for_Democratic_Institutions_and_Human_Rights" title="Office for Democratic Institutions and Human Rights">Office for Democratic Institutions and Human Rights</a>, called for election reforms in Montenegro, and for more integrity, impartiality, and professionalism in election administration.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/2019_Montenegrin_anti-corruption_protests" title="2019 Montenegrin anti-corruption protests">Protests against corruption</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Democratic_Party_of_Socialists" class="mw-redirect" title="Democratic Party of Socialists">DPS</a>-led government started in February 2019 after the revelation of footage and documents that appeared to implicate top officials in obtaining funds for the ruling party. On 30 March, all 39 opposition MPs signed an "Agreement for the Future", proposed by the protest organizers, in which they promised to boycott the 2020 elections if they were deemed irregular. In May 2020 the protest organizers called for a boycott of the 30 August elections, along with some opposition parties, claiming that the elections would not be held under fair conditions.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The EU-backed board for electoral system reform, which both the government and the opposition participated in, failed in December 2019, after the opposition left the board sessions as a protest against the government passing a controversial law on religion, accusing the ruling party of inciting ethnic hatred and unrest.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> In late December 2019 <a href="/wiki/2019%E2%80%9320_Clerical_protests_in_Montenegro" class="mw-redirect" title="2019–20 Clerical protests in Montenegro">another wave of protests started</a> against the newly adopted law which <i>de jure</i> transfers the ownership of church buildings and estates from the <a href="/wiki/Serbian_Orthodox_Church_in_Montenegro" class="mw-redirect" title="Serbian Orthodox Church in Montenegro">Serbian Orthodox Church</a> to the Montenegrin state.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In its political rights and civil liberties worldwide report in May 2020, <a href="/wiki/Freedom_House" title="Freedom House">Freedom House</a> marked Montenegro as a <a href="/wiki/Hybrid_regime" title="Hybrid regime">hybrid regime</a> rather than a democracy because of declining standards in governance, justice, elections, and media freedom. Freedom House stated that years of increasing state capture, abuse of power and <a href="/wiki/Strongman_(politics)" title="Strongman (politics)">strongman</a> tactics had been employed by long-term Prime Minister and President <a href="/wiki/Milo_%C4%90ukanovi%C4%87" title="Milo Đukanović">Milo Đukanović</a>, and for the first time since 2003, Montenegro was no longer categorised as a democracy. The report emphasised the unequal electoral process, cases of political arrests, negative developments related to judicial independence, media freedoms, as well as a series of unresolved cases of corruption within the DPS-led government. Despite repeated demands from the opposition, NGO sector, and the EU-backed institutions for the professionalization and neutralisation of institutions controlling the electoral process, they are still under the de facto control of the ruling Democratic Party of Socialists-led coalition government.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Electoral_system">Electoral system</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2020_Montenegrin_parliamentary_election&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Electoral system">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The 81 seats of the <a href="/wiki/Parliament_of_Montenegro" title="Parliament of Montenegro">Parliament of Montenegro</a> are elected in a single nationwide constituency using <a href="/wiki/Closed_list" title="Closed list">closed list</a> <a href="/wiki/Proportional_representation" title="Proportional representation">proportional representation</a>. Seats are allocated using the <a href="/wiki/D%27Hondt_method" title="D&#39;Hondt method">d'Hondt method</a> with a 3% <a href="/wiki/Electoral_threshold" title="Electoral threshold">electoral threshold</a>. However, minority groups that account for no more than 15% of the population in a <a href="/wiki/Municipalities_of_Montenegro" title="Municipalities of Montenegro">district</a> are given an exemption that lowers the electoral threshold to 0.7% for a maximum of three seats.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> A separate exemption is given to <a href="/wiki/Croats_of_Boka_Kotorska" class="mw-redirect" title="Croats of Boka Kotorska">ethnic Croats</a> whereby if no list representing the population passes the 0.7% threshold, the list with the most votes will win one seat if it receives more than 0.35% of the vote.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Campaign">Campaign</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2020_Montenegrin_parliamentary_election&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Campaign">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On 1 May 2019, the <a href="/wiki/Socialist_People%27s_Party_(Montenegro)" class="mw-redirect" title="Socialist People&#39;s Party (Montenegro)">Socialist People's Party</a> (SNP), <a href="/wiki/United_Montenegro" title="United Montenegro">United Montenegro</a> (UCG), <a href="/wiki/Workers%27_Party_(Montenegro)" title="Workers&#39; Party (Montenegro)">Workers' Party</a> (RP) and <a href="/wiki/Independent_politician" title="Independent politician">Independent group</a> in <a href="/wiki/Parliament_of_Montenegro" title="Parliament of Montenegro">the parliament</a> agreed to form a new catch-all political alliance under the name <a href="/wiki/For_the_Benefit_of_All" title="For the Benefit of All">For the Benefit of All</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> The alliance eventually dissolved prior the election. In August 2020 all three parties decided to join a pre-election coalition with right-wing <a href="/wiki/Democratic_Front_(Montenegro)" title="Democratic Front (Montenegro)">Democratic Front</a> (DF) alliance, under the name <a href="/wiki/For_the_Future_of_Montenegro" title="For the Future of Montenegro">For the Future of Montenegro</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> as did some minor political parties, such as the <a href="/wiki/True_Montenegro" title="True Montenegro">True Montenegro</a> (PCG), <a href="/wiki/Democratic_Party_of_Unity" title="Democratic Party of Unity">Democratic Party of Unity</a> (DSJ), <a href="/wiki/Democratic_Serb_Party_(Montenegro)" title="Democratic Serb Party (Montenegro)">Democratic Serb Party</a> (DSS) and <a href="/wiki/Yugoslav_Communist_Party_of_Montenegro" title="Yugoslav Communist Party of Montenegro">Yugoslav Communist Party</a> (JKP).<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On 11 July 2020 the <a href="/wiki/United_Reform_Action" title="United Reform Action">Civic Movement URA</a> decided to run independently, presenting its centre-left "<a href="/wiki/United_Reform_Action" title="United Reform Action">In Black and White</a>" <a href="/wiki/Party_platform" title="Party platform">election platform</a>, led by <a href="/wiki/Independent_candidates" class="mw-redirect" title="Independent candidates">independent candidates</a>, including well known journalist and activist <a href="/wiki/Milka_Tadi%C4%87" title="Milka Tadić">Milka Tadić</a>, some <a href="/wiki/University_of_Montenegro" title="University of Montenegro">university professors</a>, journalists, civic and NGO activists, with the party leader <a href="/wiki/Dritan_Abazovi%C4%87" title="Dritan Abazović">Dritan Abazović</a> as a ballot carrier.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> URA electoral list also contains one representative of the Bosniak minority interests <a href="/wiki/Justice_and_Reconciliation_Party" title="Justice and Reconciliation Party">SPP</a> party, as well of some minor localist parties and initiatives.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Democratic_Montenegro" title="Democratic Montenegro">Democratic Montenegro</a>, <a href="/wiki/DEMOS_(Montenegro)" title="DEMOS (Montenegro)">DEMOS</a>, the <a href="/wiki/New_Left_(Montenegro)" title="New Left (Montenegro)">New Left</a>, <a href="/wiki/Party_of_United_Pensioners_and_the_Disabled" title="Party of United Pensioners and the Disabled">PUPI</a> and the NGO <a href="/wiki/Vladimir_Pavi%C4%87evi%C4%87" title="Vladimir Pavićević">Policy Research Society</a>, agreed to form a pre-election coalition under the name <a href="/wiki/Peace_is_Our_Nation" title="Peace is Our Nation">Peace is Our Nation</a>, with Democratic Montenegro leader <a href="/wiki/Aleksa_Be%C4%8Di%C4%87" title="Aleksa Bečić">Aleksa Bečić</a> as a ballot carrier.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> 
</p><p>On 12 July 2020 the opposition <a href="/wiki/Social_Democratic_Party_of_Montenegro" title="Social Democratic Party of Montenegro">Social Democratic Party of Montenegro</a> (SDP) announced that it would run independently,<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> as did the <a href="/wiki/Social_Democrats_of_Montenegro" title="Social Democrats of Montenegro">Social Democrats of Montenegro</a> (SD), junior party in the <a href="/wiki/Markovi%C4%87_Cabinet" title="Marković Cabinet">previous government coalition</a>, a few days earlier.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On 28 July 2020 the <a href="/wiki/Albanian_List_(Montenegro)" title="Albanian List (Montenegro)">Albanian List</a>, an <a href="/wiki/Minority_politics" class="mw-redirect" title="Minority politics">minority politics</a> coalition was formed by <a href="/wiki/New_Democratic_Force_(Montenegro)" title="New Democratic Force (Montenegro)">New Democratic Force</a> (Forca), the <a href="/wiki/Albanian_Alternative" title="Albanian Alternative">Albanian Alternative</a> (AA), <a href="/wiki/Albanian_Coalition_%22Perspective%22" title="Albanian Coalition &quot;Perspective&quot;">Albanian Coalition Perspective</a> (AKP) and the <a href="/wiki/Democratic_League_in_Montenegro" title="Democratic League in Montenegro">Democratic League of Albanians</a> (DSA),<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> A few weeks earlier, <a href="/wiki/Democratic_Union_of_Albanians" title="Democratic Union of Albanians">Democratic Union of Albanians</a> (DUA) agreed to join the <a href="/wiki/Democratic_Party_(Montenegro)" title="Democratic Party (Montenegro)">DP</a> and <a href="/wiki/Democratic_League_in_Montenegro" title="Democratic League in Montenegro">DSCG</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Albanian_Coalition" title="Albanian Coalition">Albanian Coalition "Unanimously"</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Priror August 2020 the centre-right minority politics <a href="/wiki/Bosniak_Party" title="Bosniak Party">Bosniak Party</a> (BS) announced that it would run independently, as did the centre-right <a href="/wiki/Croatian_Civic_Initiative" title="Croatian Civic Initiative">Croatian Civic Initiative</a> (HGI) and the newly formed centrist <a href="/wiki/Croatian_Reform_Party" class="mw-redirect" title="Croatian Reform Party">Croatian Reform Party</a> (HRS).<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On 1 August 2020 the ruling <a href="/wiki/Democratic_Party_of_Socialists_of_Montenegro" title="Democratic Party of Socialists of Montenegro">Democratic Party of Socialists</a> (DPS) decided to run independently, with <a href="/wiki/Andrija_Popovi%C4%87" title="Andrija Popović">Andrija Popović</a> as <a href="/wiki/Liberal_Party_of_Montenegro" title="Liberal Party of Montenegro">Liberal Party</a> (LP) representative on their electoral list, with incumbent PM <a href="/wiki/Du%C5%A1ko_Markovi%C4%87" title="Duško Marković">Duško Marković</a> as a ballot carrier.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>Poll results are listed in the table below in reverse chronological order, showing the most recent first, and using the date poll was published. The highest percentage figure in each polling survey is displayed in bold, and the background shaded in the leading party's colour. In the instance that there is a tie, then no figure is shaded. The lead column on the right shows the percentage-point difference between the two parties with the highest figures. The threshold for a party to provide their deputies in parliament is 3%, except for minority parties and coalitions (E.g. <a href="/wiki/Bosniak_Party" title="Bosniak Party">BS</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_Democratic_Force_(Montenegro)" title="New Democratic Force (Montenegro)">Forca</a> and <a href="/wiki/Albanian_Alternative" title="Albanian Alternative">AA</a>), for which that threshold does not apply.

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Results">Results</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2020_Montenegrin_parliamentary_election&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Results">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The result was a victory for the lists of parties which were in opposition in the previous parliament: <a href="/wiki/For_the_Future_of_Montenegro" title="For the Future of Montenegro">For the Future of Montenegro</a>, <a href="/wiki/Peace_is_Our_Nation" title="Peace is Our Nation">Peace is Our Nation</a>, <a href="/wiki/United_Reform_Action" title="United Reform Action">United Reform Action</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Social_Democratic_Party_of_Montenegro" title="Social Democratic Party of Montenegro">Social Democratic Party</a>. Opposition lists won 41 of the 81 seats in the&#160;Parliament of Montenegro, while the ruling <a href="/wiki/Democratic_Party_of_Socialists" class="mw-redirect" title="Democratic Party of Socialists">DPS</a>, together with the <a href="/wiki/Liberal_Party_of_Montenegro" title="Liberal Party of Montenegro">Liberal Party</a>, won 30 seats, going into opposition after thirty years in power. Three opposition lists, For the Future of Montenegro, Peace is Our Nation, and the United Reform Action party, which won a combined majority of 41 MPs, announced the formation of a coalition government.<sup id="cite_ref-N1_1_31-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-N1_1-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Opposition success at the national level was accompanied by its success at <a href="/wiki/2020_Montenegrin_municipal_elections" title="2020 Montenegrin municipal elections">local level elections</a>. It won four out of five municipalities where elections were held, including <a href="/wiki/Budva" title="Budva">Budva</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kotor" title="Kotor">Kotor</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tivat" title="Tivat">Tivat</a> and <a href="/wiki/Andrijevica" title="Andrijevica">Andrijevica</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup>

<p>The <a href="/wiki/Organization_for_Security_and_Co-operation_in_Europe" title="Organization for Security and Co-operation in Europe">OSCE</a> and <a href="/wiki/Office_for_Democratic_Institutions_and_Human_Rights" title="Office for Democratic Institutions and Human Rights">ODIHR</a> announced in preliminary findings and conclusions that elections were competitive and held in an atmosphere of high polarization over issues of church and <a href="/wiki/Controversy_over_ethnic_and_linguistic_identity_in_Montenegro" title="Controversy over ethnic and linguistic identity in Montenegro">national identity</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-OSCE_2020_35-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OSCE_2020-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-France24_36-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-France24-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> They also stated that the election date was not held in accordance with the Constitution, there was no independent campaign coverage, and the ruling party profited unjustifiably through widespread abuse of office and state resources.<sup id="cite_ref-OSCE_2020_35-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OSCE_2020-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-France24_36-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-France24-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-EWB1_37-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-EWB1-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> Furthermore, they pointed out that, contrary to the national legislation and international standards, several MPs were arrested or prosecuted between elections.<sup id="cite_ref-OSCE_2020_35-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-OSCE_2020-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-EWB1_37-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-EWB1-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Political scientist <a href="/wiki/Florian_Bieber" title="Florian Bieber">Florian Bieber</a> described results as the "good news for democracy".<sup id="cite_ref-France24_36-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-France24-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Josep_Borrell" title="Josep Borrell">Josep Borrell</a>, <a href="/wiki/High_Representative_of_the_Union_for_Foreign_Affairs_and_Security_Policy" title="High Representative of the Union for Foreign Affairs and Security Policy">High Representative of the European Union for Foreign Affairs and Security Policy</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Oliv%C3%A9r_V%C3%A1rhelyi" title="Olivér Várhelyi">Olivér Várhelyi</a>, <a href="/wiki/European_Commissioner_for_Neighbourhood_and_Enlargement" title="European Commissioner for Neighbourhood and Enlargement">European Commissioner for Neighbourhood and Enlargement</a>, stated that they "look forward to the constitution of a new parliament and the formation of a new government that will continue Montenegro's steady path towards the EU."<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup> Chair of Women wing of the <a href="/wiki/European_People%27s_Party" title="European People&#39;s Party">European People's Party</a> (EPP), <a href="/wiki/Doris_Pack" title="Doris Pack">Doris Pack</a> supported the new parliamentary majority in Montenegro, criticizing the outgoing DPS regime for "socialist propaganda" tactics, she appealed the Western public and media to not fall in the trap of obvious propaganda, emphasizing that the EU path will undoubtedly remain the principal objective of the new government, after the regime change.<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Next day after the election, the leaders of two opposition electoral lists, <a href="/wiki/For_the_Future_of_Montenegro" title="For the Future of Montenegro">For the Future of Montenegro</a> and <a href="/wiki/Peace_is_Our_Nation" title="Peace is Our Nation">Peace is Our Nation</a>, as well as the <a href="/wiki/United_Reform_Action" title="United Reform Action">United Reform Action</a> party, that combined won a slim majority, agreed to form an <a href="/wiki/Technocracy" title="Technocracy">expert government</a>, to continue to work on the <a href="/wiki/Accession_of_Montenegro_to_the_European_Union" title="Accession of Montenegro to the European Union">European Union accession process</a>, fight against corruption, overcoming society polarization and economic crisis, and to work on changing the <a href="/wiki/2019-20_Clerical_protests_in_Montenegro" class="mw-redirect" title="2019-20 Clerical protests in Montenegro">disputed Law on Religious Communities</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-France24_36-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-France24-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-N1_1_31-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-N1_1-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> They also welcomed the minority parties of <a href="/wiki/Bosniaks_of_Montenegro" title="Bosniaks of Montenegro">Bosniaks</a> and <a href="/wiki/Albanians_in_Montenegro" title="Albanians in Montenegro">Albanians in Montenegro</a> and wished to form a government with them.<sup id="cite_ref-N1_1_31-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-N1_1-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-BI_42-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BI-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On 1 September, <a href="/wiki/Milo_%C4%90ukanovi%C4%87" title="Milo Đukanović">Milo Đukanović</a> conceded defeat, accusing Serbian President&#160;<a href="/wiki/Aleksandar_Vu%C4%8Di%C4%87" title="Aleksandar Vučić">Aleksandar Vučić</a>&#160;and <a href="/wiki/Media_of_Serbia" class="mw-redirect" title="Media of Serbia">Belgrade-based media</a> of interfering in the internal politics of Montenegro, as well of alleged trying to revive a "Greater Serbia&#160;policy".<sup id="cite_ref-N1_2_43-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-N1_2-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup> He stated that it is possible that his party lost its support due to dissatisfaction with some policy, but also due to manipulations from&#160;<a href="/wiki/Belgrade" title="Belgrade">Belgrade</a>, as well from the&#160;<a href="/wiki/Serbian_Orthodox_Church_in_Montenegro" class="mw-redirect" title="Serbian Orthodox Church in Montenegro">Serbian Orthodox Church in Montenegro</a>, due to the disputed Law on Religious Communities.<sup id="cite_ref-N1_2_43-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-N1_2-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Incidents">Incidents</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2020_Montenegrin_parliamentary_election&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Incidents">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Massive celebrations were held all over Montenegro the day after the elections. In Podgorica, opposition supporters clashed with the ruling DPS supporters.<sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Zdravko_Krivokapi%C4%87" title="Zdravko Krivokapić">Zdravko Krivokapić</a>, <a href="/wiki/Aleksa_Be%C4%8Di%C4%87" title="Aleksa Bečić">Aleksa Bečić</a> and <a href="/wiki/Dritan_Abazovi%C4%87" title="Dritan Abazović">Dritan Abazović</a>, the leaders of the opposition lists, called on supporters to stay at home, as well as authorities to investigate incidents and prosecute any attackers.<sup id="cite_ref-BI_42-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BI-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup> Although their <a href="/wiki/Markovi%C4%87_Cabinet" title="Marković Cabinet">incumbent cabinet</a> had previously banned all public gatherings due to the <a href="/wiki/COVID-19_pandemic_in_Montenegro" title="COVID-19 pandemic in Montenegro">COVID-19 pandemic in Montenegro</a>, the ruling <a href="/wiki/Democratic_Party_of_Socialists" class="mw-redirect" title="Democratic Party of Socialists">Democratic Party of Socialists</a> of President <a href="/wiki/Milo_%C4%90ukanovi%C4%87" title="Milo Đukanović">Milo Đukanović</a> announced a counter-meeting, due to the alleged "threat for Montenegrin statehood and independence", in case the DPS falls from a position of power.<sup id="cite_ref-47" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-47">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup> Representatives of the new parliamentary majority, including Aleksa Bečić, Zdravko Krivokapić, Dritan Abazović, <a href="/wiki/Vladimir_Jokovi%C4%87" title="Vladimir Joković">Vladimir Joković</a>, <a href="/wiki/Miodrag_Leki%C4%87" title="Miodrag Lekić">Miodrag Lekić</a> and <a href="/wiki/Vladimir_Pavi%C4%87evi%C4%87" title="Vladimir Pavićević">Vladimir Pavićević</a> strongly condemned violence and conflict, accusing the <a href="/wiki/Markovi%C4%87_Cabinet" title="Marković Cabinet">DPS-led government</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Law_enforcement_in_Montenegro" title="Law enforcement in Montenegro">Police Administration</a> and the <a href="/wiki/National_Security_Agency_(Montenegro)" title="National Security Agency (Montenegro)">National Security Agency of Montenegro</a> administration of staging conflict and&#160;inciting ethnic hatred and unrest, in order to prevent a peaceful change of decades-long regime in Montenegro.<sup id="cite_ref-48" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup> Krivokapić also demanded that the delinquents and perpetrators of the unrest must be identified and prosecuted by the police and the prosecution, as soon as possible.<sup id="cite_ref-49" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-49">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On 2 September, glass was broken on the door of the <a href="/wiki/Islam_in_Montenegro" title="Islam in Montenegro">Islamic Community</a> of <a href="/wiki/Pljevlja" title="Pljevlja">Pljevlja</a> and a message was left saying "The black bird has taken off, Pljevlja will be <a href="/wiki/Srebrenica_massacre" title="Srebrenica massacre">Srebrenica</a>." The informal group "Bunt Crna Gora" announced that, if the police do not immediately shed light on the case, "Serbs from Pljevlja will organize people's patrols in order to protect their neighbors from DPS subversion."<sup id="cite_ref-50" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-50">&#91;50&#93;</a></sup> Albin Ćeman, the deputy leader of the opposition <a href="/wiki/Democratic_Montenegro" title="Democratic Montenegro">Democrats</a> and member of Islamic community, called for calming tensions and accused the ruling party of instructed provocation.<sup id="cite_ref-51" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-51">&#91;51&#93;</a></sup> The For the Future of Montenegro coalition condemned the incidents and also expressed suspicion that it was done by the outgoing DPS-led regime.<sup id="cite_ref-52" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-52">&#91;52&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-53" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-53">&#91;53&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Imam" title="Imam">Imam</a> of Pljevlja Samir Kadribašić stated that the attacks on the property of the Islamic community were an isolated incident of hooligans and that the new parliamentary majority parties do not represent those values.<sup id="cite_ref-54" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-54">&#91;54&#93;</a></sup> Krivokapić protested with Serbian Orthodox Church priests in front of the <a href="/wiki/Husein-pa%C5%A1a%27s_Mosque" title="Husein-paša&#39;s Mosque">Husein-paša's Mosque</a>, with the message that they are ready to defend the places of worship of all religious communities in Montenegro.<sup id="cite_ref-55" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-55">&#91;55&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-56" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-56">&#91;56&#93;</a></sup> At the pro-<a href="/wiki/Markovi%C4%87_Cabinet" title="Marković Cabinet">government</a> “Patriotic gathering” in <a href="/wiki/Cetinje" title="Cetinje">Cetinje</a>, <a href="/wiki/Montenegrin_nationalism" title="Montenegrin nationalism">Montenegrin nationalists</a> performed <a href="/wiki/Anti-Serb_sentiment" title="Anti-Serb sentiment">anti-Serb</a> songs by <a href="/wiki/Miroslav_%C5%A0koro" title="Miroslav Škoro">Miroslav Škoro</a> and <i><a href="/wiki/Thompson_(band)" title="Thompson (band)">Thompson</a></i>, which also included the <a href="/wiki/Usta%C5%A1e" title="Ustaše">Ustaše</a> <a href="/wiki/Za_dom_spremni" title="Za dom spremni">salute</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-57" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-57">&#91;57&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-58" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-58">&#91;58&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-59" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-59">&#91;59&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
